Seekho IPC - XIV

Chapter XIV: Offences affecting the Public Health, Safety, Convenience, Decency, and Morals (Sections 268 to 294A)

Section 268: Public Nuisance

Description

This section defines a public nuisance as an act that causes harm, danger, or annoyance to the public at large.

Example 1

Blocking a public road with unauthorized structures can be considered a public nuisance.

Example 2

Releasing harmful pollutants into the air that affect a community falls under this section.

Section 269: Negligent Act Likely to Spread Infection of Disease Dangerous to Life

Description

This section covers any negligent act that may contribute to the spread of a dangerous infectious disease.

Example 1

Not following quarantine protocols and thereby infecting others is an offense under this section.

Example 2

Failing to report a contagious disease and exposing the public to infection is covered by this section.

Section 270: Malignant Act Likely to Spread Infection of Disease Dangerous to Life

Description

This section deals with deliberate acts intended to spread an infectious disease that could endanger life.

Example 1

Intentionally spreading a deadly virus knowing its potential consequences is punishable under this section.

Example 2

Deliberately contaminating food or water to cause an outbreak of a disease is covered by this section.

Section 271: Disobedience to Quarantine Rule

Description

This section punishes the disobedience of quarantine regulations that are in place to prevent the spread of infectious diseases.

Example 1

Violating a mandatory isolation order during an epidemic is an offense under this section.

Example 2

Refusing to adhere to government-imposed quarantine during an outbreak is covered by this section.

Section 272: Adulteration of Food or Drink Intended for Sale

Description

This section deals with the adulteration of food or drink that is meant to be sold to the public, making it harmful for consumption.

Example 1

Adding harmful chemicals to food products to increase their shelf life is an offense under this section.

Example 2

Selling adulterated milk with unsafe additives is covered by this section.

Section 273: Sale of Noxious Food or Drink

Description

This section punishes anyone who sells food or drink that is dangerous to human health.

Example 1

Selling spoiled meat to the public is an offense under this section.

Example 2

Offering beverages contaminated with toxic substances is covered by this section.

Section 274: Adulteration of Drugs

Description

This section addresses the adulteration of drugs, making them harmful or less effective for consumers.

Example 1

Diluting a drug with harmful additives to cut costs is an offense under this section.

Example 2

Mixing expired chemicals into medicines for sale is covered by this section.

Section 275: Sale of Adulterated Drugs

Description

This section covers the sale of drugs that have been adulterated, potentially causing harm to those who consume them.

Example 1

Selling drugs that contain diluted or unsafe ingredients is punishable under this section.

Example 2

Offering counterfeit medicines that fail to meet safety standards is covered by this section.

Section 276: Sale of Drug as a Different Drug or Preparation

Description

This section addresses the sale of a drug under the guise of being a different drug or preparation, misleading consumers about its true nature.

Example 1

Selling a medication labeled as a pain reliever but actually containing a different, potentially harmful substance.

Example 2

Offering a drug as a natural remedy while it contains synthetic or unauthorized ingredients.

Section 277: Fouling Water of Public Spring or Reservoir

Description

This section penalizes the act of contaminating or polluting public springs or reservoirs, which are sources of water for the community.

Example 1

Discharging industrial waste into a public reservoir is an offense under this section.

Example 2

Dumping sewage into a public spring that supplies drinking water to the community.

Section 278: Making Atmosphere Noxious to Health

Description

This section deals with actions that create an atmosphere harmful to health, such as releasing toxic fumes or pollutants into the air.

Example 1

Releasing hazardous chemicals into the air from a factory without proper filtration systems.

Example 2

Burning toxic materials that emit harmful smoke into the environment.

Section 279: Rash Driving or Riding on a Public Way

Description

This section addresses reckless driving or riding on public roads that endangers the safety of others.

Example 1

Speeding excessively in a populated area without regard for traffic rules.

Example 2

Performing dangerous maneuvers such as weaving through traffic or ignoring traffic signals.

Section 280: Rash Navigation of Vessel

Description

This section covers reckless navigation of vessels that endangers other watercraft or individuals.

Example 1

Navigating a boat at high speed in congested waterways without proper control.

Example 2

Operating a vessel under the influence of alcohol or drugs, leading to unsafe conditions.

Section 281: Exhibition of False Light, Mark or Buoy

Description

This section pertains to the display of misleading or false signals, marks, or buoys that could lead to accidents or confusion.

Example 1

Using a fake lighthouse signal to mislead ships and cause navigational hazards.

Example 2

Placing misleading navigation markers that could lead to collisions or groundings.

Section 282: Conveying Person by Water for Hire in Unsafe or Overloaded Vessel

Description

This section covers the act of transporting individuals by water in vessels that are unsafe or overloaded.

Example 1

Using a boat that is not seaworthy and carrying more passengers than it can safely handle.

Example 2

Transporting people in a vessel without proper safety equipment or measures in place.

Section 283: Danger or Obstruction in Public Way or Line of Navigation

Description

This section addresses the creation of danger or obstruction on public ways or navigational routes, impacting safety and accessibility.

Example 1

Placing hazardous materials or barriers on a public road that obstruct traffic.

Example 2

Setting up unauthorized structures or obstructions in a navigational channel.

Section 284: Negligent Conduct with Respect to Poisonous Substance

Description

This section covers negligent handling or conduct involving poisonous substances, leading to potential harm.

Example 1

Improper storage of toxic chemicals that could lead to accidental exposure or poisoning.

Example 2

Negligently spilling poisonous substances in areas accessible to the public.

Section 285: Negligent Conduct with Respect to Fire or Combustible Matter

Description

This section deals with negligent actions related to fire or combustible materials that pose a risk to safety.

Example 1

Leaving a fire unattended or improperly handling combustible materials that could lead to a fire.

Example 2

Improper disposal of flammable materials, increasing the risk of accidental ignition.

Section 286: Negligent Conduct with Respect to Explosive Substance

Description

This section covers negligent behavior involving explosive substances, which can lead to dangerous situations.

Example 1

Storing explosives in unsafe conditions that could lead to accidental detonation.

Example 2

Handling explosives without proper precautions or safety measures in place.

Section 287: Negligent Conduct with Respect to Machinery

Description

This section pertains to careless actions involving machinery that could lead to accidents or injuries.

Example 1

Operating machinery without proper safety guards or equipment, leading to potential injuries.

Example 2

Negligently maintaining machinery, causing malfunctions or hazards to operators.

Section 288: Negligent Conduct with Respect to Pulling Down or Repairing Buildings

Description

This section deals with negligent conduct during the process of demolishing or repairing buildings, leading to potential safety hazards.

Example 1

Failure to use proper supports or precautions during building demolition, risking collapse or injury.

Example 2

Negligent repair work that causes unsafe conditions or structural weaknesses in a building.

Section 289: Negligent Conduct with Respect to Animal

Description

This section covers negligent treatment or handling of animals that may endanger others or the animals themselves.

Example 1

Allowing a dangerous animal to roam freely without proper control, posing a risk to public safety.

Example 2

Neglecting proper care for animals that leads to harm or risk to their well-being.

Section 290: Punishment for Public Nuisance in Cases Not Otherwise Provided For

Description

This section addresses penalties for public nuisances not specifically covered under other provisions.

Example 1

Handling of situations causing general public inconvenience that does not fall under specific categories of nuisance.

Example 2

Addressing any form of public nuisance that disrupts community peace but is not explicitly mentioned in other sections.

Section 291: Continuance of Nuisance After Injunction to Discontinue

Description

This section covers the continuation of a nuisance after a legal injunction has been issued to stop it.

Example 1

Continuing activities that were ordered to be halted by a court due to their nuisance nature.

Example 2

Ignoring a legal order to cease a public nuisance and persisting in causing disturbance.

Section 292: Sale, etc., of Obscene Books, etc.

Description

This section deals with the sale, distribution, or public display of obscene books or materials.

Example 1

Selling books or materials that are deemed obscene by law or community standards.

Example 2

Distributing obscene content through any public or private channels.

Section 293: Sale, etc., of Obscene Objects to Young Person

Description

This section addresses the sale or distribution of obscene objects to individuals who are underage.

Example 1

Selling explicit materials to minors or persons below the legal age.

Example 2

Distributing obscene objects to young persons through various means, including online platforms.

Section 294: Obscene Acts and Songs

Description

This section pertains to the performance or display of obscene acts or songs in public places.

Example 1

Performing or singing obscene material in a public area, causing disturbance or offense.

Example 2

Publicly displaying obscene acts or songs that offend public decency and morality.

Section 294A: Keeping Lottery Office

Description

This section concerns the operation or maintenance of an office for conducting lotteries, which is subject to legal regulations.

Example 1

Operating a lottery office without the necessary legal permits or authorization.

Example 2

Maintaining a lottery office that does not comply with legal standards and regulations.
