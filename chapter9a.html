Seekho IPC - Chapter IX(A)

Chapter IX: Chapter IXA: Offences relating to Elections (Sections 171A to 171I)

Legal Sections

Section 171A: Candidate, Electoral Right Defined

Description

This section defines the terms 'candidate' and 'electoral right' in the context of elections.

Example 1

A person who formally declares their intention to run for an elected position is defined as a candidate under this section.

Example 2

An individual's right to vote in elections, as well as their ability to influence the election outcome, is defined as their electoral right.

Section 171B: Bribery

Description

This section addresses the offense of bribery in elections, detailing what constitutes bribery and its implications.

Example 1

Offering money or gifts to voters in exchange for their support is considered bribery under this section.

Example 2

A candidate providing financial incentives to election officials to influence their decisions constitutes bribery.

Section 171C: Undue Influence at Elections

Description

This section deals with undue influence exerted over voters or officials to affect the outcome of an election.

Example 1

Threatening or coercing voters to vote for a particular candidate is considered undue influence.

Example 2

Using personal relationships to pressure voters into supporting a candidate is also classified as undue influence.

Section 171D: Personation at Elections

Description

This section covers the offense of personation, where an individual pretends to be someone else in order to vote or influence the election.

Example 1

Casting a vote using someone else's identity or credentials is classified as personation.

Example 2

Attempting to influence election results by impersonating an official or election agent is also considered personation.

Section 171E: Punishment for Bribery

Description

This section outlines the penalties and punishment for those found guilty of bribery in elections.

Example 1

A candidate convicted of offering bribes to voters may face imprisonment or fines as specified under this section.

Example 2

Individuals found guilty of accepting bribes in exchange for votes may also face similar penalties.

Section 171F: Punishment for Undue Influence or Personation at an Election

Description

This section specifies the punishment for those found guilty of undue influence or personation during an election.

Example 1

A person convicted of using undue influence to sway a voter may face legal penalties such as fines or imprisonment.

Example 2

Penalties for someone caught impersonating another person to vote include potential jail time and fines.

Section 171G: False Statement in Connection with an Election

Description

This section addresses the offense of making false statements related to an election, including spreading misinformation.

Example 1

Publishing false claims about a candidate's qualifications or criminal record constitutes a false statement.

Example 2

Spreading incorrect information about election procedures or results is also covered under this section.

Section 171H: Illegal Payments in Connection with an Election

Description

This section deals with illegal payments or financial transactions made in connection with an election.

Example 1

Offering money to voters in return for their support, outside the legal campaign finance limits, is considered illegal payment.

Example 2

Any form of financial incentive provided to election officials to sway their decisions is also deemed illegal under this section.

Section 171I: Failure to Keep Election Accounts

Description

This section addresses the failure to maintain accurate and complete election accounts as required by law.

Example 1

Not recording all campaign expenditures and donations accurately in the official accounts is a violation of this section.

Example 2

Failure to submit the required financial reports to the election commission as stipulated by law is also covered by this section.
