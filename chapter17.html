Seekho IPC - XVII

Chapter XVII: Offences against Property (Sections 378 to 462)

Section 378: Theft

Description

This section defines theft, outlining the unlawful taking of property with the intent to permanently deprive the owner of it.

Example 1

Stealing someone's wallet without their knowledge or consent.

Example 2

Taking a bicycle from a public place with the intent to permanently keep it.

Section 379: Punishment for Theft

Description

This section specifies the punishment for committing theft, including imprisonment and fines.

Example 1

Imprisonment for a period as prescribed by law for stealing property.

Example 2

Fines and imprisonment for theft, depending on the severity and circumstances.

Section 380: Theft in Dwelling House, etc.

Description

This section addresses theft committed in a dwelling house, place of worship, or a place where people live.

Example 1

Stealing valuables from a house while the occupants are away.

Example 2

Theft of money from a drawer in a temple.

Section 382: Theft After Preparation Made for Causing Death, Hurt, or Restraint

Description

This section deals with theft committed after preparation for causing death, hurt, or restraint to facilitate the theft.

Example 1

Planning and preparing to cause harm to the owner to commit theft.

Example 2

Using threats of violence to subdue the victim while committing theft.

Section 383: Extortion

Description

This section defines extortion, involving obtaining property or money through coercion or threats.

Example 1

Threatening someone to force them to hand over money or property.

Example 2

Using threats of harm to extract money from a person.

Section 384: Punishment for Extortion

Description

This section outlines the punishment for committing extortion, including possible imprisonment and fines.

Example 1

Imprisonment for extortion, depending on the severity of the crime.

Example 2

Penalties for extortion, which may include fines and a prison term.

Section 385: Putting Person in Fear of Injury to Commit Extortion

Description

This section deals with putting someone in fear of injury as a means to commit extortion.

Example 1

Threatening to harm someone if they do not comply with extortion demands.

Example 2

Using threats of physical harm to coerce someone into giving up their money or valuables.

Section 386: Extortion by Putting a Person in Fear of Death or Grievous Hurt

Description

This section addresses extortion committed by threatening death or grievous hurt.

Example 1

Threatening someone with death if they do not pay money or provide property.

Example 2

Using threats of severe injury to force someone into compliance.

Section 387: Putting Person in Fear of Death or Grievous Hurt, in Order to Commit Extortion

Description

This section involves putting a person in fear of death or grievous hurt with the intent to extort property or money.

Example 1

Threatening someone with death to obtain their property.

Example 2

Coercing someone into giving up money by threatening serious bodily harm.

Section 388: Extortion by Threat of Accusation of an Offence Punishable with Death or Imprisonment for Life, etc.

Description

This section addresses extortion involving threats of accusing someone of an offence punishable with death or life imprisonment.

Example 1

Threatening to accuse someone of a serious crime to obtain money or property.

Example 2

Using threats of false accusations to coerce a person into complying with extortion demands.

Section 389: Putting Person in Fear of Accusation of Offence, in Order to Commit Extortion

Description

This section involves putting someone in fear of being accused of an offence as a means to extort money or property.

Example 1

Threatening to falsely accuse someone of a crime to extract money from them.

Example 2

Coercing a person into giving up property by threatening them with a criminal accusation.

Section 390: Robbery

Description

This section defines robbery, involving taking property from someone with the use of force or threat of force.

Example 1

Stealing money from a person while using threats or actual physical violence.

Example 2

Robbing someone on the street with a weapon.

Section 391: Dacoity

Description

This section defines dacoity, a more serious form of robbery involving a group of people committing robbery together.

Example 1

A gang of robbers attacking a bank and stealing money.

Example 2

Multiple individuals working together to rob a large jewelry store.

Section 392: Punishment for Robbery

Description

This section specifies the punishment for committing robbery, including imprisonment and fines.

Example 1

Imprisonment for robbery, as prescribed by law.

Example 2

Penalties for robbery that may include both fines and prison time.

Section 393: Attempt to Commit Robbery

Description

This section deals with attempts to commit robbery, even if the robbery was not completed.

Example 1

Attempting to rob a bank but being stopped by security before any money was taken.

Example 2

Trying to steal from a store but getting caught before completing the robbery.

Section 394: Voluntarily Causing Hurt in Committing Robbery

Description

This section covers cases where hurt is caused to a person during the commission of robbery.

Example 1

Example 2

Description

This section specifies the punishment for committing dacoity, including imprisonment and fines.

Example 2

Penalties that may include severe prison terms for participation in dacoity.
