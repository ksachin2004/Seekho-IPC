Seekho IPC - X

Chapter X: Contempt of the Lawful Authority of Public Servants (Sections 172 to 190)

Section 172: Absconding to Avoid Service of Summons or Other Proceedings

Description

This section deals with the offense of absconding to evade the service of summons or other legal proceedings.

Example 1

A person who deliberately hides from authorities to avoid receiving a court summons is committing an offense under this section.

Example 2

Fleeing the jurisdiction to avoid facing legal proceedings after being ordered to appear in court constitutes absconding.

Section 173: Preventing Service of Summons or Other Proceedings, or Preventing Publication Thereof

Description

This section addresses the offense of obstructing the service of summons or legal proceedings, or preventing their publication.

Example 1

Deliberately hiding or destroying summons to prevent it from reaching the intended recipient is covered under this section.

Example 2

Interfering with the process of publishing legal notices to obstruct their public knowledge constitutes an offense under this section.

Section 174: Non-Attendance in Obedience to an Order from Public Servant

Description

This section covers the offense of failing to attend or respond to an order issued by a public servant.

Example 1

Not appearing in court as ordered by a judge is a violation of this section.

Example 2

Failing to attend a police inquiry when summoned is also an offense under this section.

Section 174A: Non-Appearance in Response to a Proclamation under Section 82 of Act 2 of 1974

Description

This section addresses the failure to appear in response to a proclamation made under Section 82 of Act 2 of 1974.

Example 1

Ignoring a public proclamation ordering appearance in court under Section 82 of Act 2 of 1974 is an offense under this section.

Example 2

Not responding to a court proclamation regarding an order of arrest or appearance as per Act 2 of 1974 also constitutes non-appearance.

Section 175: Omission to Produce Document or Electronic Record to Public Servant by Person Legally Bound to Produce It

Description

This section deals with the offense of not producing a document or electronic record when legally required to do so by a public servant.

Example 1

Failing to provide financial records to a tax inspector when required by law is an offense under this section.

Example 2

Not producing requested legal documents to a court when legally obligated to do so constitutes omission under this section.

Section 176: Omission to Give Notice or Information to Public Servant by Person Legally Bound to Give It

Description

This section covers the offense of not providing notice or information to a public servant when legally required to do so.

Example 1

Failing to report a change of address to the local authorities when legally obligated to do so is an offense under this section.

Example 2

Not informing the police about a known crime when legally required to provide such information constitutes omission.

Section 177: Furnishing False Information

Description

This section addresses the offense of providing false information to a public servant when required to provide such information.

Example 1

Providing a false address to law enforcement during an investigation is an offense under this section.

Example 2

Giving inaccurate details about a vehicle’s registration to a public officer when requested also constitutes an offense under this section.

Section 178: Refusing Oath or Affirmation When Duly Required by Public Servant to Make It

Description

This section covers the offense of refusing to take an oath or affirmation when legally required by a public servant.

Example 1

Refusing to take an oath as a witness during a court trial when directed by the judge is an offense under this section.

Example 2

Not complying with a requirement to affirm the truthfulness of a statement during a public hearing also falls under this section.

Section 179: Refusing to Answer Public Servant Authorized to Question

Description

This section pertains to the offense of refusing to answer questions posed by a public servant who is authorized to question.

Example 1

Refusing to provide information to a police officer during an investigation when lawfully required is an offense under this section.

Example 2

Declining to answer questions from a tax official during an audit, when legally obligated, is covered by this section.

Section 180: Refusing to Sign Statement

Description

This section addresses the offense of refusing to sign a statement or document when legally required to do so by a public servant.

Example 1

Not signing a legal affidavit as instructed by a court official when required is an offense under this section.

Example 2

Refusing to sign a statement during a police interrogation when legally obliged to do so is covered by this section.

Section 181: False Statement on Oath or Affirmation to Public Servant or Person Authorized to Administer an Oath or Affirmation

Description

This section deals with making a false statement while under oath or affirmation to a public servant or person authorized to administer an oath.

Example 1

Providing false testimony under oath during a court trial is an offense under this section.

Example 2

Making misleading statements while affirming documents before a public notary is also covered under this section.

Section 182: False Information, with Intent to Cause Public Servant to Use His Lawful Power to the Injury of Another Person

Description

This section addresses the offense of providing false information with the intent to cause a public servant to misuse their power to harm another person.

Example 1

Filing a false complaint with the police to have someone wrongfully arrested is an offense under this section.

Example 2

Providing fabricated evidence to a regulatory body to initiate an unwarranted investigation against a competitor is covered by this section.

Section 183: Resistance to the Taking of Property by the Lawful Authority of a Public Servant

Description

This section deals with resisting the lawful seizure of property by a public servant authorized to take it.

Example 1

Physically obstructing a bailiff from seizing property under a court order constitutes resistance under this section.

Example 2

Refusing to hand over assets to a tax authority executing a seizure based on an unpaid tax order is also an offense under this section.

Section 184: Obstructing Sale of Property Offered for Sale by Authority of Public Servant

Description

This section addresses the offense of obstructing the sale of property that is offered for sale by a public servant’s authority.

Example 1

Interfering with a public auction of seized goods by obstructing bidders is an offense under this section.

Example 2

Blocking the sale of property confiscated for non-payment of taxes also falls under this section.

Section 185: Illegal Purchase or Bid for Property Offered for Sale by Authority of Public Servant

Description

This section deals with the offense of making illegal purchases or bids for property that is offered for sale by the authority of a public servant.

Example 1

Participating in a bid for seized property while having prior knowledge of the property’s illegal status is an offense under this section.

Example 2

Buying property from a public auction with an undisclosed conflict of interest also constitutes an offense under this section.

Section 186: Obstructing Public Servant in Discharge of Public Functions

Description

This section covers the offense of obstructing a public servant while they are performing their official duties.

Example 1

Blocking a public servant from accessing a public property during an inspection is an offense under this section.

Example 2

Physically preventing a government official from carrying out their duties at a public event is covered by this section.

Section 187: Omission to Assist Public Servant When Bound by Law to Give Assistance

Description

This section deals with the offense of failing to assist a public servant when legally required to do so.

Example 1

Not helping a police officer during an emergency situation when legally required to provide assistance is an offense under this section.

Example 2

Refusing to cooperate with a fire department official during a fire emergency when legally obligated is covered by this section.

Section 188: Disobedience to Order Duly Promulgated by Public Servant

Description

This section pertains to the offense of disobeying an order that has been properly issued by a public servant.

Example 1

Ignoring a lawful order to vacate a restricted area issued by a public official is an offense under this section.

Example 2

Failing to comply with a traffic regulation issued by a local authority is also covered by this section.

Section 189: Threat of Injury to Public Servant

Description

This section covers the offense of threatening harm to a public servant.

Example 1

Threatening physical harm to a police officer during an arrest is an offense under this section.

Example 2

Making verbal threats against a public health official while they are performing their duties is covered by this section.

Section 190: Threat of Injury to Induce Person to Refrain from Applying for Protection to Public Servant

Description

This section deals with the offense of threatening harm to a person to prevent them from seeking protection from a public servant.

Example 1

Threatening a victim of domestic violence to prevent them from seeking assistance from law enforcement is an offense under this section.

Example 2

Intimidating a whistleblower to stop them from reporting corruption to a government agency is covered by this section.
