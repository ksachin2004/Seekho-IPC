Seekho IPC - XXIII

Chapter XXIII: Attempts to Commit Offences (Sections 511)

Section 511: Punishment for Attempting to Commit Offences Punishable with Imprisonment for Life or Other Imprisonment

Description

This section outlines the punishment for individuals who attempt to commit offences that are punishable by imprisonment for life or any other term of imprisonment. It specifically addresses cases where an attempt to commit such offences has been made, but the crime was not completed.

Example 1

If a person attempts to commit murder, which is an offence punishable with life imprisonment, and fails to complete the act, they can be punished under this section for the attempt.

Example 2

A person who attempts to commit a serious offence like robbery with murder, but does not succeed in executing the crime, can still face punishment under this section for the attempted offence.
