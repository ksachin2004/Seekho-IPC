Seekho IPC - Sections 6 to 52A

Chapter I & II: General Explanations (Sections 6 to 52A)

Section 6: Definitions in the Code to be Understood Subject to Exceptions

Description

This section clarifies that definitions provided in the IPC are subject to the exceptions specified within the Code.

Example 1

A person accused of theft may be exempt from punishment if the act was done under specific legal exceptions, such as self-defense.

Example 2

An individual causing harm may not be liable under certain legal circumstances such as during a lawful arrest.

Section 7: Sense of Expression Once Explained

Description

This section ensures that terms defined or explained once will carry the same meaning throughout the Code unless explicitly stated otherwise.

Example 1

If the term "property" is defined in one section, it will hold the same meaning wherever it appears unless otherwise specified.

Example 2

The term "person" once defined will apply consistently throughout the IPC unless a different definition is provided.

Section 52A: Exception for Medical Procedures

Description

This section provides an exception for medical procedures, stating that acts done in good faith by a medical professional for the benefit of the patient are exempt from punishment.

Example 1

A doctor performing surgery that unintentionally results in a patient's death may not be criminally liable if the procedure was done in good faith.

Example 2

A dentist causing pain while extracting a tooth is not liable under IPC as it is done with the patient's benefit in mind.
