Seekho IPC - Sections 6 to 52A

Chapter I & II: General Explanations (Sections 6 to 52A)

Section 6: Definitions in the Code to be Understood Subject to Exceptions

Description

This section clarifies that definitions provided in the IPC are subject to the exceptions specified within the Code.

Example 1

A person accused of theft may be exempt from punishment if the act was done under specific legal exceptions, such as self-defense.

Example 2

An individual causing harm may not be liable under certain legal circumstances such as during a lawful arrest.

Section 7: Sense of Expression Once Explained

Description

This section ensures that terms defined or explained once will carry the same meaning throughout the Code unless explicitly stated otherwise.

Example 1

If the term "property" is defined in one section, it will hold the same meaning wherever it appears unless otherwise specified.

Example 2

The term "person" once defined will apply consistently throughout the IPC unless a different definition is provided.

Section 8: Gender

Description

This section clarifies that the pronouns he and she refer to any person, regardless of gender.

Example 1

In legal terms, "he" can refer to both male and female offenders.

Example 2

The term "he" in a legal document can apply to a female individual as well, ensuring inclusivity.

Section 9: Number

Description

This section explains that singular words shall include the plural, and vice versa, unless specifically stated otherwise.

Example 1

The word "person" can refer to multiple persons as well, depending on the context.

Example 2

The term "offense" in singular form may cover more than one offense in specific cases.

Section 10: Man and Woman

Description

This section defines "man" as a male of any age and "woman" as a female of any age.

Example 1

A newborn girl would legally be considered a "woman" under this section.

Example 2

An elderly male would be classified as a "man" under the IPC provisions.

Section 11: Person

Description

"Person" includes any company or association or body of persons, whether incorporated or not.

Example 1

A corporation can be charged under the IPC for any offense committed.

Example 2

An association of individuals can be treated as a "person" under the Code.

Section 12: Public

Description

"Public" includes any class of the public or any community.

Example 1

A group of individuals gathered in a park can be considered "public."

Example 2

An online forum can represent a "public" community under this section.

Section 13: Queen

Description

This section applies to the sovereign, signifying "Queen" in any part of the IPC.

Example 1

Any reference to the "Queen" means the ruling sovereign of that period.

Example 2

Laws referring to the "Queen" extend to cover the ruling monarch.

Section 14: Servant of Government

Description

This section defines anyone in the service of the government as a "servant of the government."

Example 1

A police officer is considered a servant of the government.

Example 2

A government school teacher is also classified as a servant of the government.

Section 52A: Exception for Medical Procedures

Description

This section provides an exception for medical procedures, stating that acts done in good faith by a medical professional for the benefit of the patient are exempt from punishment.

Example 1

A doctor performing surgery that unintentionally results in a patient's death may not be criminally liable if the procedure was done in good faith.

Example 2

A dentist causing pain while extracting a tooth is not liable under IPC as it is done with the patient's benefit in mind.
