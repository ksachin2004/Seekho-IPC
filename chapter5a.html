Seekho IPC - Chapter V(A)

Chapter VA: Criminal Conspiracy (Sections 120A to 120B)

Section 120A: Definition of Criminal Conspiracy

Description

This section provides the definition of criminal conspiracy, detailing what constitutes an agreement between two or more individuals to commit an illegal act and the legal implications of such a conspiracy.

Example 1

If two or more people plan and agree to commit a bank robbery, their agreement constitutes a criminal conspiracy under this section.

Example 2

A group of individuals who plan to engage in a coordinated scheme to commit fraud can be charged with criminal conspiracy as defined in this section.

Section 120B: Punishment of Criminal Conspiracy

Description

This section outlines the punishment for individuals involved in criminal conspiracy. It specifies the legal consequences for those who engage in a conspiracy to commit an offense, detailing the penalties that apply based on the nature of the offense and the extent of involvement in the conspiracy.

Example 1

If a group of individuals conspires to commit a serious crime like terrorism, the penalties for those involved can include severe imprisonment terms or even capital punishment, depending on the jurisdiction and the nature of the conspiracy.

Example 2

In a case where individuals conspire to commit fraud, the punishment may involve significant prison sentences and fines for all parties involved in the conspiracy, reflecting the severity of the planned offense and the actual impact of their actions.
