Seekho IPC - Chapter VII

Chapter VII: Offences relating to the Army, Navy, and Air Force (Sections 131 to 140)

Section 131: Abetting Mutiny, or Attempting to Seduce a Soldier, Sailor, or Airman from His Duty

Description

This section deals with the crime of abetting mutiny or attempting to seduce a soldier, sailor, or airman from their duty. It specifies the legal repercussions for individuals involved in such actions.

Example 1

A civilian inciting military personnel to mutiny against their superiors would be charged under this section.

Example 2

Attempting to persuade a soldier to abandon their post could lead to prosecution under this section.

Section 132: Abetment of Mutiny, If Mutiny is Committed in Consequence Thereof

Description

This section addresses the abetment of mutiny, specifying that if the mutiny is committed as a result of such abetment, the person who abetted it is liable for punishment.

Example 1

A person who encourages soldiers to mutiny, resulting in actual mutiny, would be prosecuted under this section.

Example 2

A conspirator who instigates a naval mutiny, and the mutiny occurs, falls under this section.

Section 133: Abetment of Assault by Soldier, Sailor, or Airman on His Superior Officer, When in Execution of His Office

Description

This section deals with abetment of assault by a soldier, sailor, or airman on their superior officer while the officer is performing their official duties.

Example 1

A person inciting a soldier to attack a superior officer during a military operation is covered under this section.

Example 2

Encouraging an airman to assault their commanding officer while in the line of duty would be prosecuted under this section.

Section 134: Abetment of Such Assault, If the Assault is Committed

Description

This section addresses the abetment of assault, specifically if the assault is actually committed following such abetment. It specifies the penalties for the abettor in such cases.

Example 1

An individual who encourages a sailor to assault a superior officer and the assault occurs would be charged under this section.

Example 2

A person inciting an airman to attack their commanding officer, where the attack is carried out, is liable under this section.

Section 135: Abetment of Desertion of Soldier, Sailor, or Airman

Description

This section deals with the abetment of desertion by a soldier, sailor, or airman. It specifies the penalties for those who encourage or assist in the desertion of military personnel.

Example 1

A person assisting a soldier in fleeing from their duties would be prosecuted under this section.

Example 2

Encouraging a sailor to abandon their post and providing resources to help them escape would fall under this section.

Section 136: Harbouring Deserter

Description

This section addresses the crime of harboring a deserter. It specifies the legal repercussions for individuals who provide shelter or support to military deserters.

Example 1

Providing shelter to a deserter from the armed forces would be covered under this section.

Example 2

Offering financial support or hiding a deserter from military authorities would also fall under this section.

Section 137: Deserter Concealed on Board Merchant Vessel Through Negligence of Master

Description

This section deals with the offense of a master of a merchant vessel negligently allowing a deserter to be concealed on their ship. It specifies the penalties for such negligence.

Example 1

A ship's captain who fails to check for and report the presence of a military deserter aboard is covered under this section.

Example 2

Negligence in not reporting a deserter who hides on a vessel during port checks would be prosecuted under this section.

Section 138: Abetment of Act of Insubordination by Soldier, Sailor, or Airman

Description

This section addresses the crime of abetting acts of insubordination by military personnel. It specifies the legal consequences for individuals who encourage or assist in such behavior.

Example 1

Encouraging a soldier to disobey orders from a superior officer would be prosecuted under this section.

Example 2

Assisting a sailor in engaging in acts of defiance against their commanding officer is covered under this section.

Section 138A: Application of Foregoing Sections to the Indian Marine Service

Description

This section specifies that the provisions relating to abetment of offenses by soldiers, sailors, and airmen also apply to members of the Indian Marine Service.

Example 1

The same rules apply to members of the Indian Marine Service who commit or abet offenses as outlined in Sections 131 to 138.

Example 2

Insubordination or desertion by personnel of the Indian Marine Service is subject to the same penalties as those for soldiers, sailors, and airmen.

Section 139: Persons Subject to Certain Acts

Description

This section addresses the applicability of certain laws to specific persons, detailing which individuals are subject to these laws and under what circumstances.

Example 1

Military personnel involved in specific types of conduct are subject to laws detailed in this section.

Example 2

Personnel of particular services or roles who engage in certain activities fall under the purview of these laws.

Section 140: Wearing Garb or Carrying Token Used by Soldier, Sailor, or Airman

Description

This section deals with the offense of wearing a uniform or carrying symbols used by soldiers, sailors, or airmen without authorization. It specifies the penalties for such acts.

Example 1

A civilian wearing a military uniform without permission would be prosecuted under this section.

Example 2

Carrying insignia or tokens meant for military personnel without authorization is covered under this section.
