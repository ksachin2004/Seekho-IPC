Seekho IPC - XVI

Chapter XVI: Offences affecting the Human Body (Sections 299 to 377)

Section 299: Culpable Homicide

Description

This section defines culpable homicide as the act of causing death with the intention to cause death or with knowledge that death is likely to result.

Example 1

A person intentionally assaults another with a weapon, knowing that it could result in death, and the victim dies as a result.

Example 2

Administering poison with the intent to kill, even if the death does not occur immediately.

Section 300: Murder

Description

This section defines murder as the intentional killing of another person with malice aforethought.

Example 1

A person plans and executes the killing of another person with premeditation and intent.

Example 2

A person kills another during the commission of a serious crime, such as robbery, with the intention to kill.

Section 301: Culpable Homicide by Causing Death of Person Other Than Person Whose Death Was Intended

Description

This section deals with cases where a person causes the death of someone other than the person they intended to kill, but still with the same criminal intent.

Example 1

A person attempts to kill their rival but accidentally kills an innocent bystander instead.

Example 2

Shooting at an intended target but hitting and killing someone else in the vicinity.

Section 302: Punishment for Murder

Description

This section prescribes the punishment for the offense of murder, which may include life imprisonment or death penalty, depending on the severity of the crime and the judicial discretion.

Example 1

A person convicted of murder may be sentenced to life imprisonment without parole.

Example 2

A person convicted of a particularly heinous murder may face the death penalty, as per the court's ruling.

Section 303: Punishment for Murder by Life-Convict

Description

This section deals with the punishment for a life convict who commits murder while serving their sentence. The penalty may be more severe compared to a regular murder conviction.

Example 1

A prisoner serving a life sentence for a previous crime commits murder in prison, and the court imposes an additional penalty.

Example 2

An inmate serving a life sentence for a different offense murders a fellow inmate, leading to an additional severe sentence.

Section 304: Punishment for Culpable Homicide Not Amounting to Murder

Description

This section prescribes the punishment for culpable homicide that does not meet the criteria of murder. The punishment is less severe compared to murder.

Example 1

Causing death in a situation where the intent to kill was not present, but there was a gross negligence leading to death.

Example 2

Killing someone during a heated argument without premeditation, where the act was impulsive rather than planned.

Section 304A: Causing Death by Negligence

Description

This section addresses cases where death is caused by criminal negligence, without any intention to kill, but where negligence is so gross that it results in death.

Example 1

A driver causes an accident resulting in death due to reckless driving or not following traffic rules.

Example 2

An employer's negligence leads to a worker's death due to unsafe working conditions that were not addressed.

Section 304B: Dowry Death

Description

This section deals with deaths that occur due to dowry-related harassment or violence, and the punishment for those responsible for such deaths.

Example 1

A woman dies due to burns inflicted by her husband and in-laws over dowry demands.

Example 2
