Seekho IPC - XII

Chapter XII: Offences relating to Coins and Government Stamps (Sections 230 to 263A)

Section 230: Coin Defined

Description

This section defines what constitutes a coin for legal purposes.

Example 1

A metallic disc issued by a government or authorized entity for use as currency.

Example 2

A coin that is standardized in terms of weight and value by the issuing authority.

Section 231: Counterfeiting Coin

Description

This section deals with the act of counterfeiting coins, which involves creating fake coins to deceive others.

Example 1

Using a mold to create fake coins that look like legitimate currency.

Example 2

Producing coins with the intent to pass them off as legal tender.

Section 232: Counterfeiting Indian Coin

Description

This section pertains specifically to counterfeiting coins issued by India.

Example 1

Creating fake Indian rupees with the intention of passing them as real currency.

Example 2

Producing counterfeit Indian coins with the same design and markings as legitimate coins.

Section 233: Making or Selling Instrument for Counterfeiting Coin

Description

This section addresses the creation or sale of tools and equipment used for counterfeiting coins.

Example 1

Selling a mold used for producing fake coins.

Example 2

Manufacturing dies for stamping counterfeit coins.

Section 234: Making or Selling Instrument for Counterfeiting Indian Coin

Description

This section focuses on the making or selling of instruments specifically for counterfeiting Indian coins.

Example 1

Creating dies specifically for counterfeiting Indian rupee coins.

Example 2

Selling a machine used to produce fake Indian currency coins.

Section 235: Possession of Instrument, or Material for the Purpose of Using the Same for Counterfeiting Coin

Description

This section addresses the illegal possession of tools or materials intended for counterfeiting coins.

Example 1

Possessing metal stamps and molds with the intent to create counterfeit coins.

Example 2

Holding materials used to manufacture fake coins, such as special inks or dies.

Section 236: Abetting in India the Counterfeiting Out of India of Coin

Description

This section covers the act of assisting or encouraging the counterfeiting of coins outside India.

Example 1

Providing resources or information to aid in the production of counterfeit coins in another country.

Example 2

Facilitating the transfer of counterfeit coin-making equipment to a foreign location.

Section 237: Import or Export of Counterfeit Coin

Description

This section addresses the illegal act of importing or exporting counterfeit coins.

Example 1

Bringing counterfeit coins into a country with the intent to use or distribute them.

Example 2

Shipping fake coins abroad, intending to sell or distribute them internationally.

Section 238: Import or Export of Counterfeits of Indian Coin

Description

This section focuses on the illegal import or export of counterfeit Indian coins specifically.

Example 1

Importing counterfeit Indian rupee coins into the country.

Example 2

Exporting fake Indian coins to other countries.

Section 239: Delivery of Coin, Possessed with Knowledge that It Is Counterfeit

Description

This section addresses the act of delivering a coin when the deliverer knows it is counterfeit.

Example 1

Passing off a fake coin as real currency, knowing its true nature.

Example 2

Selling a counterfeit coin while being aware that it is not genuine.

Section 240: Delivery of Indian Coin, Possessed with Knowledge that It Is Counterfeit

Description

This section covers the delivery of Indian coins that are known to be counterfeit by the deliverer.

Example 1

Providing a counterfeit Indian rupee as real currency.

Example 2

Delivering fake Indian coins while being aware of their fraudulent nature.

Section 241: Delivery of Coin as Genuine, Which, When First Possessed, the Deliverer Did Not Know to Be Counterfeit

Description

This section discusses the delivery of a coin that was initially not known to be counterfeit by the deliverer.

Example 1

Delivering a coin that was thought to be genuine at the time of receipt, but later found to be counterfeit.

Example 2

Passing a coin that was originally received as real currency but later discovered to be fake.

Section 242: Possession of Counterfeit Coin by Person Who Knew It to Be Counterfeit When He Became Possessed Thereof

Description

This section addresses the possession of counterfeit coins by someone who was aware of their fraudulent nature when they acquired them.

Example 1

Holding onto a counterfeit coin while knowing it is not genuine from the time of acquisition.

Example 2

Possessing a fake coin with full awareness of its counterfeit status at the time of possession.

Section 243: Possession of Indian Coin by Person Who Knew It to Be Counterfeit When He Became Possessed Thereof

Description

This section pertains to the possession of counterfeit Indian coins by someone who was aware of their fake status at the time of acquisition.

Example 1

Holding counterfeit Indian rupee coins with prior knowledge of their fraudulent nature.

Example 2

Possessing fake Indian coins, aware of their counterfeit status from the moment of receipt.

Section 244: Person Employed in Mint Causing Coin to Be of Different Weight or Composition from That Fixed by Law

Description

This section addresses the actions of someone employed at a mint who intentionally alters the weight or composition of coins from what is legally prescribed.

Example 1

A mint worker deliberately reduces the weight of coins to save costs or for fraudulent purposes.

Example 2

Altering the composition of coins to make them less valuable than legally required.

Section 245: Unlawfully Taking Coining Instrument from Mint

Description

This section deals with the unlawful removal of coining instruments from a mint.

Example 1

Stealing coining presses or dies from a mint.

Example 2

Removing coining tools from the mint without authorization.

Section 246: Fraudulently or Dishonestly Diminishing Weight or Altering Composition of Coin

Description

This section covers fraudulent or dishonest actions that involve reducing the weight or changing the composition of a coin.

Example 1

Melt down coins to reduce their weight and then re-mint them with less valuable metal.

Example 2

Altering the metal composition of coins to make them less valuable.

Section 247: Fraudulently or Dishonestly Diminishing Weight or Altering Composition of Indian Coin

Description

This section deals specifically with fraudulent or dishonest acts concerning the weight or composition of Indian coins.

Example 1

Reducing the weight of Indian rupee coins to make a profit.

Example 2

Altering the composition of Indian coins to decrease their value.

Section 248: Altering Appearance of Coin with Intent that It Shall Pass as Coin of Different Description

Description

This section concerns the alteration of a coin’s appearance to make it resemble a coin of a different description.

Example 1

Modifying a low-value coin to make it appear as a high-value coin.

Example 2

Changing the design of a coin to make it pass as a different type of currency.

Section 249: Altering Appearance of Indian Coin with Intent that It Shall Pass as Coin of Different Description

Description

This section addresses the alteration of the appearance of Indian coins to make them seem like a different type of coin.

Example 1

Altering Indian rupee coins to make them resemble higher denomination coins.

Example 2

Changing the appearance of Indian coins to pass them off as different types of currency.

Section 250: Delivery of Coin, Possessed with Knowledge that It Is Altered

Description

This section pertains to the delivery of a coin that the deliverer knows has been altered.

Example 1

Delivering a coin with knowledge that its appearance has been changed.

Example 2

Passing off an altered coin as genuine currency while knowing its altered state.

Section 251: Delivery of Indian Coin, Possessed with Knowledge that It Is Altered

Description

This section deals with the delivery of Indian coins that the deliverer knows have been altered.

Example 1

Delivering an Indian rupee coin with knowledge of its altered condition.

Example 2

Passing off altered Indian coins as genuine while being aware of their fraudulent nature.

Section 252: Possession of Coin by Person Who Knew It to Be Altered When He Became Possessed Thereof

Description

This section pertains to the possession of altered coins by someone who was aware of their changed state when they acquired them.

Example 1

Holding a coin that has been altered, knowing its changed state at the time of possession.

Example 2

Possessing altered currency while being aware of its fraudulent condition.

Section 253: Fraudulently Altering Coin

Description

This section addresses the act of fraudulently altering a coin.

Example 1

Tampering with coins to change their value or appearance fraudulently.

Example 2

Altering coins to deceive others about their value.

Section 254: Fraudulently Altering Indian Coin

Description

This section concerns the fraudulent alteration of Indian coins.

Example 1

Tampering with Indian currency to change its value or appearance fraudulently.

Example 2

Altering Indian coins with the intent to deceive others regarding their value.

Section 255: Selling Coin Which Is Known to Be Fraudulently Altered

Description

This section involves selling coins that are known to be fraudulently altered.

Example 1

Selling coins that have been fraudulently altered while being aware of their condition.

Example 2

Trading coins that are known to be tampered with to mislead buyers.

Section 256: Selling Indian Coin Which Is Known to Be Fraudulently Altered

Description

This section covers the sale of Indian coins that are known to be fraudulently altered.

Example 1

Selling altered Indian currency while being aware of its tampered state.

Example 2

Distributing Indian coins with knowledge that they have been fraudulently altered.

Section 257: Keeping Indian Coin with Knowledge that It Is Fraudulently Altered

Description

This section deals with the possession of Indian coins known to be fraudulently altered.

Example 1

Keeping Indian coins with knowledge of their fraudulent alteration.

Example 2

Storing Indian currency that has been tampered with while being aware of its altered state.

Section 258: Making or Providing Equipment for Altering Coin

Description

This section concerns making or providing tools or equipment used for altering coins.

Example 1

Creating or supplying equipment used for the fraudulent alteration of coins.

Example 2

Providing tools that enable the tampering or counterfeiting of coins.

Section 259: Making or Providing Equipment for Altering Indian Coin

Description

This section pertains to making or providing tools specifically for altering Indian coins.

Example 1

Creating or supplying tools used to fraudulently alter Indian currency.

Example 2

Providing equipment that facilitates the tampering of Indian coins.

Section 260: Using as Genuine a Government Stamp Known to Be a Counterfeit

Description

This section addresses the use of a counterfeit Government stamp as if it were genuine.

Example 1

Using a forged Government stamp on a legal document to mislead authorities.

Example 2

Submitting a document with a counterfeit Government stamp to evade fees or taxes.

Section 261: Effacing, Writing from Substance Bearing Government Stamp, or Removing from Document a Stamp Used for It, with Intent to Cause Loss to Government

Description

This section covers the act of removing or altering a Government stamp from a document with the intent to cause financial loss to the Government.

Example 1

Effacing a stamp from a document to reuse it fraudulently, causing financial loss to the Government.

Example 2

Removing a Government stamp from a document and then using it on another document to evade duties.

Section 262: Using Government Stamp Known to Have Been Before Used

Description

This section addresses the use of a Government stamp that is known to have been previously used.

Example 1

Using a previously used Government stamp on a new document to falsely represent it as properly stamped.

Example 2

Submitting a document with an old Government stamp that has already been canceled or used.

Section 263: Erasure of Mark Denoting That Stamp Has Been Used

Description

This section deals with the act of erasing or removing marks that indicate a Government stamp has been used.

Example 1

Erasing the cancellation mark on a stamp to reuse it fraudulently.

Example 2

Removing marks from a stamp on a document to make it appear as if it were unused.

Section 263A: Prohibition of Fictitious Stamps

Description

This section prohibits the creation or use of fictitious Government stamps.

Example 1

Creating fake Government stamps for fraudulent use.

Example 2

Using stamps that were not issued by the Government but are falsely represented as such.
