Seekho IPC - XI

Chapter XI: False Evidence and Offences against Public Justice (Sections 191 to 229A)

Section 191: Giving False Evidence

Description

This section addresses the offense of giving false evidence in legal proceedings.

Example 1

Testifying to facts that you know to be untrue during a court trial is an offense under this section.

Example 2

Providing false information in a sworn affidavit is also covered by this section.

Section 192: Fabricating False Evidence

Description

This section covers the offense of fabricating false evidence with the intent to mislead a court.

Example 1

Creating or altering documents to misrepresent facts in a legal case is an offense under this section.

Example 2

Manufacturing false evidence, such as fake photographs or forged signatures, for use in court proceedings is covered by this section.

Section 193: Punishment for False Evidence

Description

This section specifies the punishment for giving false evidence in legal proceedings.

Example 1

A person convicted of providing false testimony may face imprisonment or fines as per this section.

Example 2

Providing false evidence that affects the outcome of a trial could result in a penalty under this section.

Section 194: Giving or Fabricating False Evidence with Intent to Procure Conviction of Capital Offence

Description

This section deals with giving or fabricating false evidence with the intent to secure a conviction for a capital offense.

Example 1

Fabricating evidence to wrongfully convict someone of a capital crime, such as murder, is covered by this section.

Example 2

Providing false witness statements to ensure a death penalty conviction is an offense under this section.

Section 195: Giving or Fabricating False Evidence with Intent to Procure Conviction of Offence Punishable with Imprisonment for Life or Imprisonment

Description

This section pertains to giving or fabricating false evidence with the intent to secure a conviction for an offense punishable by life imprisonment or other imprisonment.

Example 1

Creating false evidence to ensure a life sentence conviction for a serious crime is an offense under this section.

Example 2

Providing false testimony to lead to a conviction that results in a long-term prison sentence is covered by this section.

Section 195A: Threatening Any Person to Give False Evidence

Description

This section covers the offense of threatening a person to induce them to give false evidence.

Example 1

Threatening a witness to coerce them into providing false testimony is an offense under this section.

Example 2

Using intimidation to force someone to lie in court is covered by this section.

Section 196: Using Evidence Known to be False

Description

This section deals with the offense of using evidence that you know to be false in legal proceedings.

Example 1

Submitting false documents in court that you know are not authentic is an offense under this section.

Example 2

Presenting false witness statements knowing they are untrue is covered by this section.

Section 197: Issuing or Signing False Certificate

Description

This section addresses the offense of issuing or signing a certificate that is known to be false.

Example 1

Signing a medical certificate with false information is an offense under this section.

Example 2

Issuing a fake academic certificate for fraudulent purposes is covered by this section.

Section 198: Using as True a Certificate Known to be False

Description

This section involves using a certificate that you know is false as if it were true.

Example 1

Presenting a fake certificate to gain employment or benefits is an offense under this section.

Example 2

Using a falsified document for legal or financial transactions is covered by this section.

Section 199: False Statement Made in Declaration Which is by Law Receivable as Evidence

Description

This section addresses making false statements in a declaration that is legally receivable as evidence.

Example 1

Making a false statement in a legal declaration or affidavit that is accepted as evidence in court is an offense under this section.

Example 2

Submitting a false declaration in legal proceedings, knowing it will be used as evidence, is covered by this section.

Section 200: Using as True Such Declaration Knowing It to be False

Description

This section deals with using a declaration that you know is false as if it were true.

Example 1

Using a false declaration to obtain legal advantages or benefits is an offense under this section.

Example 2

Submitting false information in a legal document with the intent to deceive is covered by this section.

Section 201: Causing Disappearance of Evidence of Offence, or Giving False Information to Screen Offender

Description

This section covers causing evidence of an offense to disappear or providing false information to hide an offender.

Example 1

Destroying physical evidence to prevent it from being used in court is an offense under this section.

Example 2

Providing false information to authorities to prevent the arrest of a criminal is covered by this section.

Section 202: Intentional Omission to Give Information of Offence by Person Bound to Inform

Description

This section addresses the intentional omission to provide information about an offense by someone who is legally obligated to inform.

Example 1

Failing to report a crime to the authorities when you are legally required to do so is an offense under this section.

Example 2

Not informing law enforcement about an offense you witnessed, despite being legally bound to report it, is covered by this section.

Section 203: Giving False Information Respecting an Offence Committed

Description

This section pertains to giving false information about an offense that has been committed.

Example 1

Falsely reporting a crime or providing inaccurate details about it to law enforcement is an offense under this section.

Example 2

Providing misleading information about an offense to hinder the investigation is covered by this section.

Section 204: Destruction of Document or Electronic Record to Prevent Its Production as Evidence

Description

This section deals with the destruction of documents or electronic records to prevent their production as evidence.

Example 1

Destroying emails or files that are relevant to a legal case to prevent them from being used as evidence is an offense under this section.

Example 2

Eliminating physical documents that could be used as evidence in a trial is covered by this section.

Section 205: False Personation for Purpose of Act or Proceeding in Suit or Prosecution

Description

This section addresses false personation for the purpose of participating in an act or proceeding in a suit or prosecution.

Example 1

Pretending to be someone else to participate in legal proceedings or to influence a legal process is an offense under this section.

Example 2

Impersonating a witness or a party in a legal case to affect the outcome is covered by this section.

Section 206: Fraudulent Removal or Concealment of Property to Prevent Its Seizure as Forfeited or in Execution

Description

This section covers the fraudulent removal or concealment of property to prevent its seizure as forfeited or in execution.

Example 1

Hiding or relocating property to avoid its seizure by authorities is an offense under this section.

Example 2

Concealing assets to prevent their attachment during legal proceedings is covered by this section.

Section 207: Fraudulent Claim to Property to Prevent Its Seizure as Forfeited or in Execution

Description

This section addresses fraudulent claims to property to avoid its seizure as forfeited or in execution.

Example 1

Making false claims of ownership to prevent property from being seized during legal proceedings is an offense under this section.

Example 2

Claiming property as yours to obstruct its legal seizure is covered by this section.

Section 208: Fraudulently Suffering Decree for Sum Not Due

Description

This section covers the act of fraudulently causing a decree for an amount that is not actually due.

Example 1

Obtaining a court decree for a debt amount that is inflated or not owed is an offense under this section.

Example 2

Manipulating legal proceedings to obtain a decree for an amount not actually due is covered by this section.

Section 209: Dishonestly Making False Claim in Court

Description

This section deals with making false claims dishonestly in a court proceeding.

Example 1

Submitting false evidence or making dishonest claims in a court case is an offense under this section.

Example 2

Making false statements or claims to deceive the court is covered by this section.

Section 210: Fraudulently Obtaining Decree for Sum Not Due

Description

This section addresses the fraudulent obtaining of a decree for a sum that is not actually due.

Example 1

Obtaining a legal decree for an amount that is not owed through deceitful means is an offense under this section.

Example 2

Using fraudulent tactics to secure a decree for a non-existent debt is covered by this section.

Section 211: False Charge of Offence Made with Intent to Injure

Description

This section pertains to making a false charge of an offense with the intent to harm or injure another person.

Example 1

Falsely accusing someone of a crime to cause them harm or damage is an offense under this section.

Example 2

Making a false accusation to tarnish someone’s reputation or to get them into trouble is covered by this section.

Section 212: Harbouring Offender

Description

This section deals with the act of harboring an offender, which includes hiding or sheltering someone who has committed a crime.

Example 1

Allowing a fugitive to stay at your residence to avoid arrest constitutes harboring an offender.

Example 2

Providing financial support to someone on the run from law enforcement agencies to evade capture.

Section 213: Taking Gift, etc., to Screen an Offender from Punishment

Description

This section concerns the act of accepting a gift or other benefits in exchange for protecting an offender from punishment.

Example 1

Accepting a bribe to prevent a criminal from being prosecuted or punished is an offense under this section.

Example 2

Receiving monetary gifts from an offender or their associates to avoid reporting them to the authorities.

Section 214: Offering Gift or Restoration of Property in Consideration of Screening Offender

Description

This section covers the act of offering gifts or returning property in exchange for helping an offender avoid legal consequences.

Example 1

Offering money or valuables to a public official to ignore a criminal's actions or delay their prosecution.

Example 2

Restoring seized property to an offender in exchange for not reporting their criminal activities.

Section 215: Taking Gift to Help to Recover Stolen Property, etc.

Description

This section addresses taking gifts or bribes to aid in the recovery of stolen property or similar matters.

Example 1

Accepting money to help locate or return stolen property to the thief instead of to its rightful owner.

Example 2

Receiving bribes to manipulate investigations or reports regarding stolen goods.

Section 216: Harbouring Offender Who Has Escaped from Custody or Whose Apprehension Has Been Ordered

Description

This section pertains to the act of harboring an offender who has escaped from custody or is subject to an order for their apprehension.

Example 1

Providing a hiding place to a fugitive who has escaped from jail or is being actively pursued by law enforcement.

Example 2

Sheltering an individual who is under a legal order for arrest to prevent their capture by authorities.

Section 217: Public Servant Disobeying Direction of Law with Intent to Save Person from Punishment or Property from Forfeiture

Description

This section deals with public servants who intentionally disobey legal directions to protect a person from punishment or to prevent the forfeiture of property.

Example 1

A government official deliberately ignoring legal procedures to help a friend avoid legal consequences.

Example 2

A public servant failing to enforce the seizure of property as required by law to protect a person from losing their assets.

Section 218: Public Servant Framing Incorrect Record or Writing with Intent to Save Person from Punishment or Property from Forfeiture

Description

This section involves public servants who create false records or documents with the intent to save a person from punishment or to protect property from forfeiture.

Example 1

Altered or falsified official documents to prevent legal action against an individual.

Example 2

Creating or modifying records to falsely show compliance with legal requirements to avoid penalties.

Section 216A: Penalty for Harbouring Robbers or Dacoits

Description

This section specifies the penalties for harboring robbers or dacoits, addressing the criminal consequences for providing refuge to these individuals.

Example 1

Harboring individuals who have committed armed robbery and are evading capture.

Example 2

Providing shelter to a gang of robbers who are being actively pursued by law enforcement.

Section 216B: Definition of “Harbour” in Sections 212, 216 and 216A

Description

This section defines what constitutes “harbouring” under the mentioned sections, providing clarity on the scope of the term in a legal context.

Example 1

“Harbouring” includes not only physical shelter but also providing financial support or other forms of aid to evade justice.

Example 2

Aiding in the escape or evasion of a person from law enforcement, such as hiding their whereabouts or helping them avoid capture.

Section 219: Public Servant in Judicial Proceeding Corruptly Making Report, etc., Contrary to Law

Description

This section involves public servants who make false or corrupt reports during judicial proceedings, contrary to legal requirements.

Example 1

Submitting falsified evidence or reports during a court case to influence the outcome improperly.

Example 2

A judicial officer knowingly providing misleading information to affect the proceedings unlawfully.

Section 220: Commitment for Trial or Confinement by Person Having Authority Who Knows That He Is Acting Contrary to Law

Description

This section addresses the commitment of individuals for trial or confinement by someone with authority who is aware they are acting against the law.

Example 1

A police officer detaining a person without proper legal grounds, knowing their action is unlawful.

Example 2

An authority figure sending someone for trial despite knowing there is no legal basis for the charge.

Section 221: Intentional Omission to Apprehend on the Part of Public Servant Bound to Apprehend

Description

This section involves a public servant intentionally failing to apprehend someone they are legally obligated to arrest.

Example 1

A law enforcement officer knowingly ignoring an arrest warrant for a person they are required to detain.

Example 2

A public official deliberately avoiding the arrest of a person who has been legally ordered to be apprehended.

Section 222: Intentional Omission to Apprehend on the Part of Public Servant Bound to Apprehend Person Under Sentence or Lawfully Committed

Description

This section covers the intentional failure of a public servant to apprehend a person under sentence or who has been lawfully committed.

Example 1

A correctional officer neglecting to take action on a prisoner who is under a legal sentence, despite being required to do so.

Example 2

A public servant failing to carry out the commitment of a person who has been legally ordered to be detained.

Section 223: Escape from Confinement or Custody Negligently Suffered by Public Servant

Description

This section deals with the negligent actions of public servants that lead to the escape of individuals from confinement or custody.

Example 1

A jailer failing to secure a prison cell properly, resulting in a detainee escaping.

Example 2

Negligence by a law enforcement officer in maintaining proper custody of a suspect, leading to their escape.

Section 224: Resistance or Obstruction by a Person to His Lawful Apprehension

Description

This section covers the act of resisting or obstructing one's own lawful apprehension by authorities.

Example 1

A person physically fighting against arresting officers who are trying to apprehend them legally.

Example 2

Obstructing law enforcement officers from carrying out a lawful arrest by fleeing or using force.

Section 225: Resistance or Obstruction to Lawful Apprehension of Another Person

Description

This section addresses the act of resisting or obstructing the lawful apprehension of another person.

Example 1

Intervening physically or verbally to prevent the arrest of another individual by law enforcement officers.

Example 2

Helping a friend evade capture by distracting or obstructing the police during an arrest attempt.

Section 226: Unlawful Return from Transportation

Description

This section deals with the illegal return of a person who has been sentenced to transportation and has returned unlawfully.

Example 1

A person who was sentenced to transportation to a penal colony returns to the country without lawful permission.

Example 2

An individual unlawfully escaping from a place of transportation and returning to their original location.

Section 227: Violation of Condition of Remission of Punishment

Description

This section addresses the violation of conditions set forth for the remission of punishment, including breaking the terms of conditional release.

Example 1

Failing to adhere to the conditions set for early release on probation.

Example 2

A person violating the terms of their conditional pardon or parole.

Section 228: Intentional Insult or Interruption to Public Servant Sitting in Judicial Proceeding

Description

This section covers the intentional insult or interruption directed at a public servant during a judicial proceeding.

Example 1

Disrupting a court session by insulting or otherwise interrupting a judge or other judicial officer.

Example 2

Verbal abuse or disruptive behavior aimed at a public servant during legal proceedings.

Section 228A: Disclosure of Identity of the Victim of Certain Offences

Description

This section addresses the unauthorized disclosure of the identity of victims in certain offenses, such as sexual crimes.

Example 1

Publishing the name or details of a victim of a sexual assault case without their consent.

Example 2

Revealing the identity of a victim of domestic violence in media reports or public statements.

Section 229: Personation of a Juror or Assessor

Description

This section deals with the act of personating a juror or assessor in a judicial proceeding, including pretending to be someone eligible to serve in that role.

Example 1

Falsely presenting oneself as a juror to gain access to confidential court proceedings.

Example 2

Impersonating an assessor in a trial to influence the judicial process unlawfully.

Section 229A: Failure by Person Released on Bail or Bond to Appear in Court

Description

This section addresses the failure of an individual who has been released on bail or bond to appear in court as required.

Example 1

A person who skips a scheduled court date after being released on bail, leading to a warrant for their arrest.

Example 2

Missing a court appearance despite having signed a bond to ensure their presence.
