Seekho IPC - XXI

Chapter XXI: Defamation (Sections 499 to 502)

Section 499: Defamation

Description

This section defines defamation, which involves harming the reputation of another person by making false statements. The defamation can be in written or spoken form and is intended to lower the person in the eyes of others.

Example 1

A person spreading false rumors about a colleague to damage their professional reputation.

Example 2

A defamatory article published in a newspaper that harms an individual's reputation.

Section 500: Punishment for Defamation

Description

This section prescribes the punishment for the offence of defamation as defined in Section 499. It includes imprisonment, which may extend to two years, or a fine, or both.

Example 1

A person convicted of defamation may face imprisonment for up to two years or a monetary fine, depending on the severity of the offence.

Example 2

The court may impose a fine on a person found guilty of defamation in addition to, or instead of, imprisonment.

Section 501: Printing or Engraving Matter Known to Be Defamatory

Description

This section deals with the offence of printing or engraving matter known to be defamatory. It applies to those who intentionally print or engrave defamatory content.

Example 1

A publisher who prints defamatory material knowing it will harm someone's reputation is liable under this section.

Example 2

An engraver who creates defamatory engravings with the intent to publish them can be charged under this section.

Section 502: Sale of Printed or Engraved Substance Containing Defamatory Matter

Description

This section addresses the sale of any printed or engraved substance that contains defamatory matter. It criminalizes the act of selling such defamatory material.

Example 1

A bookstore selling pamphlets or books containing defamatory statements about an individual is committing an offence under this section.

Example 2

A vendor selling newspapers or magazines that contain defamatory content can be prosecuted under this section.
