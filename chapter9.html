Seekho IPC - Chapter IX

Chapter IX: Offences by or relating to Public Servants (Sections 161 to 171)

Sections 161-165A: Repealed

Description

Sections 161 through 165A have been repealed and are no longer in effect.

Example 1

No current legal implications as these sections are no longer applicable.

Example 2

Historical references only, with no present legal standing or application.

Section 166: Public Servant Disobeying Law, with Intent to Cause Injury to Any Person

Description

This section deals with the offence of a public servant willfully disobeying the law to cause harm or injury to an individual.

Example 1

A police officer deliberately ignoring a legal order to investigate a crime with the intent to let the perpetrator escape.

Example 2

A regulatory official intentionally overlooking a violation of safety standards to harm a business competitor.

Section 167: Public Servant Forming an Incorrect Document with Intent to Cause Injury

Description

This section covers the act of a public servant intentionally creating or falsifying a document to cause harm or injury.

Example 1

A government clerk intentionally making false entries in an official record to harm an individualâ€™s reputation.

Example 2

A tax official creating a forged document to falsely accuse a taxpayer of evasion, intending to cause financial harm.

Section 168: Public Servant Unlawfully Engaging in Trade

Description

This section addresses the issue of a public servant engaging in trade or business activities in violation of their duties or legal restrictions.

Example 1

A municipal officer running a side business selling construction materials, creating a conflict of interest with their official duties.

Example 2

A public servant engaging in real estate transactions that affect their official capacity or decisions.

Section 169: Public Servant Unlawfully Buying or Bidding for Property

Description

This section deals with the offence of a public servant unlawfully buying or bidding for property, in breach of their official duties or restrictions.

Example 1

A government official participating in an auction for a piece of land that falls under their jurisdiction.

Example 2

A public servant making a private purchase of a property that was part of a government project or land acquisition.

Section 170: Personating a Public Servant

Description

This section addresses the crime of impersonating a public servant, including the legal consequences for pretending to be one.

Example 1

A person dressing up as a police officer and using the disguise to extort money from the public.

Example 2

An individual pretending to be a government inspector to gain unauthorized access to premises and demand bribes.

Section 171: Wearing Garb or Carrying Token Used by Public Servant with Fraudulent Intent

Description

This section covers the act of wearing attire or carrying symbols associated with public servants with fraudulent intent to deceive or commit fraud.

Example 1

A person wearing a fake government badge to gain special access or privileges they are not entitled to.

Example 2

An individual using a counterfeit official ID to impersonate a public servant and commit fraud.
